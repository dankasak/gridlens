The short version

Electricity plans are genuinely complicated — time-of-use windows, seasonal rates, daily caps, controlled-load tariffs, VPP terms, promotional credits. Grid Lens models all of this as accurately as we can, but between the growing complexity of some offers, rebates and promotions that don't fit cleanly into a rate model, the odd inaccuracy in how your inverter or meter reports usage, and — being honest — ordinary software bugs on our part, we can't promise the numbers we show will match your actual bill to the cent. Treat Grid Lens as a pointer: it tells you which plans deserve a closer look, not a guarantee of exactly what you'll pay. Always confirm the details that matter to you against the retailer's own price fact sheet before switching.

What Grid Lens is

Grid Lens is a Home Assistant integration and companion API that compares electricity retail plans against your own energy usage, and estimates what you'd pay on alternative plans. All of this runs from data our servers hold about available plans, combined with usage data that stays inside your own Home Assistant instance — see our Privacy Policy for exactly what that involves.
